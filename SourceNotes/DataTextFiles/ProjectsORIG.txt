Undergraduate and graduate students in AMS HST 435/535 in Fall 2017, Fall 2018, and Fall 2019 completed the research and database records you can use here. This is an ongoing project.&lt;/p&gt;  &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Description of Project&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;  &lt;p&gt;Beginning in 2017, students in Public History Practicum (AMS HST 435/535) began systematically recording all students who attended Miami University in the years 1824 to 1864. Students were recorded by name and variants of name, hometown, state, and other information appearing with the name entry. In 2018 students completed this segment of the project. In 2019 students began recording the student names appearing in the ledgers, rosters, and other handwritten materials related to the various literary societies, covering the years 1824 to 1864.&lt;/p&gt;  &lt;p&gt;The names of all students who attended Miami University in the years between 1824, the first year classes were held at this University founded in 1809, to 1864, the final year of the Civil War. Up to 30% of Miami Students in these years came from southern, slave-holding states, although not all Southern students owned enslaved human beings, nor came from slave-owning families. However, one goal of this project is to allow for further research into the holdings of the MU students and their families. Preliminary research from students contributing to this project have found, using geneological records, census and slave-ownership censuses, family histories, and property records, that several students from southern states did come from families whose wealth was derived from the ownership of human beings.
